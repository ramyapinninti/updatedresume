Assignment 4

Global Food Metrics, Nutritional Traps, & Ecological Footprint Analysis

Launch Project Space

Abstract & Methodology Summary

Abstract: This research explores the critical intersections between global consumer food production volumes, macro-nutritional density, and environmental degradation metrics. Using a global food composition dataset, this project isolates structural friction points across the top ten highest-volume English food groups—specifically targeting how staple foods introduce dietary penalties (the "Sugar Trap") alongside ecological supply chain footprints (Eco-Score Grades D and E).

Methodology: Cross-sectional product data was systematically filtered using string matching and ASCII verification to restrict observations to the top 10 verified English food group categories. Standardized macro-nutritional distributions (carbohydrates, sugars, fats, proteins) per 100 grams were aggregated and mapped against independent evaluation scales, including the categorical Nutri-Score and continuous Eco-Score (0–100) distributions. Insights were evaluated utilizing linear regression modeling to analyze dietary asset tradeoffs, and translated into an interactive executive dashboard and exploratory narrative storyboard within Tableau to map corporate product reformulation pathways.
